Guide

Why Convert Scanned PDF to Searchable Text?

Scanned documents are essentially images. You cannot select text, search for keywords (Ctrl+F), or copy content from them. This is where OCR (Optical Character Recognition) comes in.

What is OCR?

OCR is a technology that recognizes text within a digital image. It is commonly used to recognize text in scanned documents and images. Our tool uses advanced browser-based OCR to create an invisible text layer over your image, making it look exactly like the original but with functional text.

Benefits of Searchable PDFs

  • Searchability: Find specific words or phrases instantly using search functions.
  • Accessibility: Screen readers can read the text aloud for visually impaired users.
  • Copy & Paste: Easily extract quotes or data without retyping manually.
  • Small Size: Often reduces file size compared to high-res raw scans.

Note: This tool runs entirely in your browser. Your sensitive documents are never uploaded to our servers, ensuring 100% privacy.

Does it support handwriting?

OCR works best with printed text (books, invoices, papers). Handwriting recognition may vary depending on clarity.

Which languages are supported?

Currently, we support English, Hindi, Spanish, French, and German. Select your language from the dropdown for best accuracy.

Is my document safe?

Absolutely. We use Client-side OCR. Your file is processed by your own computer's browser and is never sent to any external server.
